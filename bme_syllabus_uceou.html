🎯Programme Vision, Mission & Objectives
Vision
To coherently work with medical professionals in providing effective and affordable healthcare.
Mission
Produce biomedical engineering graduates who can apply basic engineering principles to solve medical field problems, and develop engineers who conceive innovative strategies for designing medical equipment, implants, and devices of value to society.
Key Outcomes
Engineering knowledge applied to complex medical problems Design solutions for medical devices & systems Effective communication with healthcare professionals Research & lifelong learning capability Ethics, teamwork, and project management skills
Programme-Specific Goals
Graduates are trained to (1) interact with healthcare professionals and medical R&D institutions to solve real clinical problems, and (2) indigenize medical products through start-ups to provide affordable healthcare in India.

🔭Elective & Research Specialisation Areas (Faculty-Led)
Signal Processing
EEG/EOG analysis, drowsiness detection, movement disorders, cognitive sciences, neural signal processing, gait analysis for Parkinson's disease patients — active faculty research areas she could join for her final-year project.
Medical Imaging
MRI innovations, X-ray, ultrasound, retinal imaging (OCTA), cancer detection via AI/machine learning — UCE has a dedicated "Professor of Practice" specialising in MRI/Radiology.
Biomaterials & Tissue Engineering
Scaffold design (Luffa-based composites), wound healing materials, bone tissue engineering, biocompatible nanomaterials for cancer therapy (hyperthermia using nano-ferrites).
Assistive & Rehabilitation Devices
Robotic arms for tele-surgery, gaze-tracking wheelchair control, smart shoes for gait monitoring, orthosis design, electronic travel aids for visually impaired persons using auditory feedback.
AI in Healthcare
Machine learning for cancer diagnosis, lung nodule detection, diabetic retinopathy screening, brain tumor classification — strong recent publication record (2023-25) in this emerging area.
Biomedical Waste & Sustainability
Classification and degradation methods for biomedical waste, nanoparticle-based microbiome growth inhibitors for healthcare waste treatment.
📝Project Work, Internship & Evaluation Structure
Hospital exposure
Department has active MoUs and research collaboration with NIMS, Apollo, CARE, Yashoda, Gandhi Hospital, DDH&RC (Durgabai Deshmukh Hospital). Several adjunct/visiting faculty are MD doctors from Osmania Medical College who teach Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry directly.
BMIC (Biomedical Instrumentation Centre)
A unique consultancy wing where students get exposure to real equipment calibration and testing work — has executed paid consultancy orders for NABH equipment calibration at Lotus Hospitals and Rise Hospitals, generating real revenue (₹2.35 lakh in one instance).
Final year project
BE Project (Phase I in Sem VII, Phase II in Sem VIII) — past student projects include: pupilometer for pupillary defect detection, eye-movement-based wheelchair control, IoT-based blood glucose monitors, AI-based cancer detection systems. 540 BE project reports archived in dept library.
Lateral entry option
6 seats available via TS-ECET for diploma holders to join directly in 2nd year — relevant if exploring alternate entry routes for any future reference.
Honors & Minors tracks
UCE offers separate "BE (Honors)" and "BE (Minors)" syllabus tracks from Semester V onward — additional credit-bearing specialisation options beyond the core BME syllabus, useful for building a distinctive profile for graduate school applications in the USA.
